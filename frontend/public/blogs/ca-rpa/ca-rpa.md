    6. Wire Transfer Fraud Warning
        1. Wire transfer fraud is an increasingly common way for hackers to steal funds, especially in real estate transactions.  Buyer and Seller are advised to confirm all wire transfer instructions by phone with the escrow holder before sending funds.  One way hackers steal funds is by sending an email that appears to be from the escrow holder with wire instructions.  To protect your funds, always confirm instructions by calling the escrow holder (using the trusted contact number for the escrow holder) before sending any funds.

7. Buyer’s Due Diligence
    1. Buyer has a responsibility to perform due diligence by investigating the Property for any issues.  This can include, but is not limited to, inspections such as a general home inspection, wood-destroying pest inspection, and lead-based paint inspection.
    2. Buyer and hired inspector(s) agree to respect the Property during the inspection(s) and Seller agrees to make the Property available for Buyer and their inspector(s) to perform the inspection(s) with proper notice.  There shall be good faith cooperation between parties to ensure Buyer has the time to complete all necessary inspections.
8. Contingencies
    1. If any of the following contingencies are not satisfied, either party has the right to cancel this agreement:
        1. If Buyer requires use of a mortgage loan, Buyer is able to successfully acquire one at terms favorable to Buyer.
        2. The value of the Property determined by a licensed appraiser is not less than the Purchase Price.
        3. Buyer accepts the Property’s condition after completing Buyer’s investigation.
        4. Buyer reviews and accepts the preliminary title report prepared by title company.
            1. Seller shall provide the preliminary title report to Buyer within 7 days of Agreement Date.
        5. Buyer reviews and accepts all disclosures listed in Section 5 (‘Disclosures’).
        6. Buyer is able to sell another property located at [property address].
    2. Unless otherwise stated, Buyer shall remove all contingencies within 17 days of Agreement Date.
        1. Buyer shall remove contingencies by sending Seller a written and signed statement indicating which contingencies to remove.
        2. If Buyer has not removed all contingencies by the dates agreed upon, Seller has the right to cancel the agreement under the following conditions:
            1. Seller has provided a written notice to Buyer to remove the contingencies within 3 days (notice must be provided no earlier than 3 days before the contingencies were originally agreed to be removed by).
            2. Buyer still fails to remove those contingencies after receiving written notice.
    3. In the event either party cancels this agreement due to unsatisfied contingencies or Buyer’s failure to remove contingencies by the dates agreed upon, Earnest Money is returned to Buyer.

    2. Both parties acknowledge that each party has the opportunity to hire professional representation to review or negotiate the agreement.
12. Cancellation
    1. In order to cancel this agreement, either party must submit a notice of cancellation in writing to the other party.
    2. Mutual cancellation: if both parties agree to mutually cancel this agreement, they shall sign a written agreement and deliver it to escrow and Earnest Money shall be returned to Buyer.
    3. Failure to perform: if either party fails to perform their duties outlined in this agreement 3 days after receiving a notice to perform by the other party, the other party has the right to cancel this agreement and Earnest Money shall be returned to Buyer.
    4. Failure to close
        1. If Buyer fails to close escrow after removing all contingencies and receiving a 3 day notice to close by Seller or if Buyer refuses to remove contingencies in bad faith, Earnest Money shall be delivered to Seller.
        2. If Seller fails to close escrow, Earnest Money shall be returned to Buyer.
    5. Earnest Money is not released from escrow until both parties have signed a written agreement indicating the rightful rightful recipient of Earnest Money.  Failure of either party to sign such an agreement in bad faith may result in a penalty up to $1000 (see California Civil Code § 1057.3 for more information).

13. Conflict Resolution
    1. Both parties agree to first attempt to settle any disputes via mediation.  The parties shall mutually select the mediation provider in the state of [state] and if parties fail to agree on a mediator within 2 days of the conflict, either party may request the assistance of a mediation service to select a provider.  Any mediation fees shall be split evenly between both parties.
    2. If mediation fails, both parties agree to settle the dispute via arbitration in the state of [state] by a mutually agreed upon provider.  **THE DECISION OF THE ARBITRATOR SHALL BE FINAL AND BINDING UPON BOTH PARTIES.**  The prevailing party is entitled to attorney fees only if that party agreed to settle the dispute via mediation initially.
